TASK I. VERB PATTERNS

Sometimes, we use two verbs together in a sentence. The second verb must follow a specific pattern: it will either be a Gerund (-ing form) or an Infinitive (to + base verb).

What is the difference?

  • Gerunds (-ing): Often focus on the experience, an action that is already happening, or a past memory.
  • Infinitives (to + verb): Often focus on a future goal, a purpose, or an action that needs to be completed.

Tricky Verbs: The verbs stop, forget, and try change their meaning depending on whether you use a gerund or an infinitive after them!

1. The Verb "Stop"

  • Stop + Gerund (verb-ing): To quit an action completely.
    Example: You should stop smoking it’s not good for your health.
  • Stop + Infinitive (to + verb): To pause one action in order to do something else.
    Example: The workers will stop to have lunch at twelve o’clock.

2. The Verb "Forget"

  • Forget + Gerund (verb-ing): Forgetting a past memory or an action you already did.
    Example: I forgot locking the door, but I’m sure I must have locked it.
  • Forget + Infinitive (to + verb): Forgetting to do a task you were supposed to do.
    Example: Oh no! I forgot to bring my running shoes.

3. The Verb "Try"

  • Try + Gerund (verb-ing): Doing something as an experiment to see if it solves a problem.
    Example: I tried doing yoga, it improved my flexibility and balance.
  • Try + Infinitive (to + verb): Attempting something difficult that requires effort.
    Example: Tanjiro tried to stop Muzan, but it was too late.

TASK II. PREPOSITIONAL PHRASE

A prepositional phrase begins with a preposition and ends with a noun or pronoun. They add extra detail to a sentence, like showing time, place, or condition.

Word Bank Matching: We can combine prepositions (by, in, for) with specific nouns (ONCE, THE END, INSTANCE, MYSELF, EXAMPLE, CHANCE) to create common phrases.

Preposition Noun Prepositional Phrase
For Once For once (meaning: just this one time)
In The End In the end (meaning: finally, after a long time)
For Instance / Example For instance / For example (meaning: as an illustration)
By Myself By myself (meaning: alone, without help)
By Chance By chance (meaning: accidentally, without planning)
