Grammar Core Rules & Formulas

Presentation Page 2 of 2 — Structural Patterns Summary

1. After Adjectives Infinitive

  • Rule: When a verb immediately follows an adjective, we use the infinitive form.
  • Examples:
    • difficult to find (c)
    • shocked to discover (d)
    • careful to avoid (g)

2. To Express Purpose Infinitive

  • Rule: Use the infinitive when answering the question "Why?" to explain the objective behind an action.
  • Examples:
    • Taken... to hang up (b)
    • Went to rooms... to have a shower (j)

3. After Prepositions Gerund

  • Rule: All prepositions (including words describing time context) structurally require an -ing verb form.
  • Examples:
    • After losing (i)
    • for brushing (k)
    • on hitting (l)

4. After Specific Verbs Mixed Types

  • Verbs of Choice/Orders: Take the infinitive (decided to have [a], asked to come [e], told to be [f]).
  • Verbs of Avoidance: Take the gerund (avoid getting [h]).
  • Aspectual Verbs (Start/Begin): Can accept either structure cleanly without modifying the base meaning (began to change / changing [m]).
