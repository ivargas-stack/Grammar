The Setting

London at Night

A big city with many lights. It is cold and very different from Ethiopia.

Key Note: Character 1 sees magic, but his father sees danger.

Where are the Stars?

The character asks: "Where are the stars?"

In the city, the lights are so bright that the stars disappear. This is Light Pollution.

"You need the dark to see the light."

A Big Secret!

Dramatic Irony

This is when WE know something that the character DOES NOT know.

Character 1: "I love this holiday!"
(He doesn't know his dad is leaving him).

Independent Writing Guide

Your Script Structure

Now it's your turn to create. Follow these steps to write a professional drama scene:

  • Step 1: Start with a Scene Heading (Where are they?).
  • Step 2: Use Stage Directions in brackets (Actions).
  • Step 3: Mix Open Questions to keep the conversation going.
  • Step 4: Add Sound Effects to show emotion.
Example Idea: Character 1 is looking for a hidden star. Character 2 is worried about the time.
