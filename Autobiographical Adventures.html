UNIT 3:

AUTOBIOGRAPHICAL ADVENTURES

Player 1: Grade 7 English

LEVEL 1: THE BASICS

Welcome to Level 1! Today, we are unlocking the secrets of the Autobiography.

  • Auto: Self
  • Bio: Life
  • Graphy: Writing

An autobiographical adventure is a true story about your own life. It focuses on a specific, exciting, or meaningful event.

Rule #1: You are the main character! (First-Person Perspective).

LEVEL 2: PLAYER STATS

To survive this level, you need the right grammar mechanics!

  • Narrative Voice: First-Person. Use pronouns like I, me, my, we, our.
  • Tense: Mostly Past Tense. Because the adventure already happened!
  • Example: "I sprinted as fast as I could, but the dog was catching up to me."

Pro-Tip: Share your internal thoughts. How did you feel when you lost the boss battle or won the race?

LEVEL 3: RENDERING THE WORLD

Don't leave your reader in a blank, unrendered world. Use Sensory Imagery to build the map!

Describe your adventure using the 5 Senses:

  • See: "The blinding neon lights of the arcade..."
  • Hear: "The deafening crunch of the snow..."
  • Feel: "The rough, freezing bark of the tree..."
  • Smell: "The sharp scent of pine needles..."

LEVEL 4: TIME TRAVEL

Autobiographies usually follow a chronological sequence (the order in which events happened).

To guide your reader through the timeline, equip these Time Connectives:

  • Start: Initially, At first, To begin with...
  • Middle: Suddenly, Meanwhile, Shortly after, As soon as...
  • End: Eventually, Finally, Looking back...

BOSS STAGE: YOUR TURN!

MISSION: Write an autobiographical account of a time you tried something completely new (e.g., a new sport, moving to a new school, trying a strange food).

SUCCESS CRITERIA:

  • Use First-Person pronouns (I, my).
  • Maintain consistent Past Tense.
  • Use at least 3 varied Time Connectives.
  • Include 2 pieces of Sensory Imagery.
  • End with a reflection: What did you learn?
