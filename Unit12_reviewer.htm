1. Delexical Verbs (Collocations)

Delexical verbs are high-frequency verbs that lose their primary meaning to the noun they are paired with. Choosing the right verb depends on the noun that follows.
VerbMeaning / ContextCommon Nouns
TakeAction or resta break, a rest, a test, a look, a turn, a seat
HavePossession of experiencea rest, a drink, a chat, a meal, a laugh, a go
GoMovement or Change in statecrazy, bad, missing, online, wrong, blind
DoActivity or Workhomework, a job, the dishes, business, sport
MakeCreation or resulta mess, a mistake, a noise, a speech, a deal

2. Speech Sounds

In dramatic writing, these sounds (Onomatopoeia) describe character reactions or environmental noises without needing long narration.
  • Phew!: Expresses relief or being hot.
  • Ugh!: Expresses disgust or horror.
  • Uh-oh!: Used when a mistake is realized or danger is ahead.
  • Er... / Hmm...: Shows hesitation, thinking, or doubt.
  • SPLAT!: Sound of something wet or messy hitting a surface.
  • Clatter!: Sound of hard, metal objects falling or crashing.

3. Question Types

Questions in English serve different communicative purposes beyond just asking for info.
TypePurposeExample
SubjectAsks about the 'doer'Who loves you? / Who broke the vase?
RhetoricalShows emotion/sarcasmHow should I know? / Are you serious?
TagConfirms informationYou're not leaving, are you? / It's hot, isn't it?
DeclarativeStatement as questionYou're taking the bus home? / You found it?
PermissionAsking for allowanceCan I come? / May I leave the room?
