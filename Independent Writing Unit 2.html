Writing Task Unit 2

Today's Learning Objectives

  • Plan: Structure a piece of writing effectively.
  • Draft: Use specific grammar techniques from Unit 2.
  • Refine: Evaluate and edit our independent writing.

Step 1: Unpack the Prompt

Before you write a single word, read the prompt carefully. Let's identify our P.A.F.:

  • Purpose: Why are you writing? (To entertain? Inform? Describe?)
  • Audience: Who will read this? (Teacher, peers, general audience?)
  • Format: What type of text is it? (Story, recount, article?)

Step 2: Map It Out (Planning)

Don't just start writing! Use this basic structure to guide your thoughts:

  • Paragraph 1: The Hook. Set the scene and introduce the main subject. Grab the reader's attention!
  • Paragraphs 2 & 3: The Details. Build the core of your writing. Use sensory language (sight, sound, touch).
  • Paragraph 4: The Conclusion. Wrap up your thoughts. How does it end? What is the final reflection?

Steps 4 & 5: Review and Refine

The Grammar Checklist:

  • Did I use a variety of sentence lengths?
  • Are my verbs strong and precise?
  • Did I use correctly punctuated descriptive clauses?

📝 Peer Review: "Two Stars and a Wish"
Swap with a partner. Give two compliments (stars) and one area for improvement (wish). Then, polish your final draft!
